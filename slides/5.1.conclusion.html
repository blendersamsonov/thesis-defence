The main results

  • An asymptotic theory of charged particle motion in the strongly radiation-dominated regime is developed. Some general properties of particle motion in this regime are revealed, which are significantly different from the ones in the regime of weak radiation reaction.
  • A new method for approximate solution of motion equations with account of radiation reaction is demonstrated. It is validated by reproducing known solutions in several EM field configurations, such as uniform rotating parallel electric and magnetic fields, fields of model linear accelerator, and plane waves.
  • A new effect is discovered and qualitatively described, namely development of a self-sustaining QED cascade in a plane wave. It is shown that such a cascade can develop during an interaction of extremely intensive laser pulse with thin solid target. An analytical model describing the process is developed and validated by direct comparison with results of full-scale 3D QED-PIC simulations.
  • A model is developed for calculating disruption parameter determining head-on collision of high-current beams of ultrarelativistic particles which account radiation reaction (beamstrahlung). The model is verified by comparing its predictions with results of full-scale 3D QED-PIC simulations. Impact of beamstrahlung on collision of beams with parameters expected at the next generation of accelerators such as ILC, CLIC and FACET-II is estimated.
  • Using full-scale 3D QED-PIC simulations an efficient scheme of gamma radiation generation during beam-plasma interaction is demonstrated. An analytical model is developed for calculating efficiency of conversion of the beam energy to gamma radiation energy. Optimal beam parameters for FACET-II facility are found with regard to the efficiency of generation of gamma radiation.
  • A new scheme is developed and implemented into QUILL code for solving Maxwell's equations on a regular grid with significantly reduced numerical Cherenkov instability. This scheme can be used for simulation of ultrarelativistic flows of charged particles.
