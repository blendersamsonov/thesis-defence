Head-on collision of ultrarelativistic particle beams

Assumptions

  • Unifor charge distribution \[\begin{equation} n(\mathbf{r})=\begin{cases} n_e,\ |x|\leq x_b\land r\leq r_b,\\ 0,\ |x|> x_b\lor r> r_b, \end{cases} \end{equation} \]
  • Ultrarelativism ($\gamma\gg 1$)
Electric fields can be calculated from Gauss's law \[\begin{equation} E_r=2\pi e n_e\begin{cases} r,\ ,r\leq r_b,\\ r_b^2/r,\ r> r_b \end{cases} \end{equation} \] $$B_\theta = vE_r\approx E_r$$

Force from the own fields

$$F_{int} = E_r - B_\theta \propto E_r/\gamma^2 \ll E_r$$

Force from the fields of the counter-propagating beam

$$F_{ext} = E_r + B_\theta \approx 2 E_r$$

Head-on collision of ultrarelativistic particle beams

Particle motion in the field of the counter-propagating beam

$$\frac{\mathrm{d}p}{\mathrm{d}t} = -mc\omega_b^2 y$$

Normalizing to $\omega_b$

$$\frac{\mathrm{d}y}{\mathrm{d}t} = -\frac{y}{\gamma}$$

If $\gamma=\text{const}$, $y=y_0\cos\frac{t}{\sqrt{\gamma}}$ –– betatron oscillations

"Focusing" time: $t_p = \sqrt{2\gamma}$

Disruption parameter:

$$D_0 = \left( \frac{t_{int}}{t_p} \right)^2 = \frac{1}{2\gamma} \left( \frac{\omega_b\sigma_x}{c} \right)^2$$

Head-on collision of ultrarelativistic particle beams

Account of radiation reaction:

$$\frac{\mathrm{d}y}{\mathrm{d}t} = -\frac{y}{\gamma}$$ $$\frac{\mathrm{d}\gamma}{\mathrm{d}t} = -F_{rr}(\chi)$$

Characteristic timescales:

$T_D\sim\sqrt{2\gamma_0}$ –– betatron oscillations period

$T_{BS} \sim \frac{\gamma_0}{F_{rr}(\chi_0)}$ –– energy depletion timescale

$$\varkappa = \frac{T_D}{T_{BS}} = \sqrt{\frac{2}{\gamma_0}} F_{rr}(\chi_0)$$

$\varkappa \gg 1$ –– beamstrahlung dominated regime

$\varkappa \ll 1$ –– weak radiation reaction regime
