Radiation formation length$$\lambda_f = \lambda / a_0 \ll \lambda$$

Particle dynamics in the strongly radiation dominated regime

Ultrarelativistic charged particle (electron) motion equations with account of radiation reaction
$$\frac{\mathrm{d}\mathbf{v}}{\mathrm{d}t} = -\frac{\mathbf{E}+\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B} - \mathbf{v} (\mathbf{v} \mathbf{E})}{\gamma} \qquad\qquad \frac{\mathrm{d}\gamma}{\mathrm{d}t} = \mathbf{vE} - \mathbf{F}_\mathrm{rr}\left( |\mathbf{F}_\perp| \right)$$

In constant EM fields the equations are solved analytically$^1$:

  • In the reference frame $\mathcal{K}'$, in which $\mathbf{E}'\parallel\mathbf{B}'\parallel \mathbf{z}_0'$, electron rotates in the $x'y'$ plane, and its velocity along the $z'$ axis approaches 1 on a timescale $\gamma_0' mc/eE'$.
  • In the laboratory reference frame the direction $\mathbf{v}_0$ of the electric field in $\mathcal{K}'$ can be found from the equation $$\mathbf{E}+\mathbf{v}_0 \times \mathbf{B} - \mathbf{v}_0 (\mathbf{v}_0 \mathbf{E})=0$$
In the radiation dominated regime, defined with the relation $\gamma \ll eE/mc\omega\equiv a_0$, on a timescale $\gamma mc/eE\ll \omega^{-1}$ EM field can be considered constant. Thus, electron orients along the direction $\mathbf{v}_0$ defined by local fields, faster than EM fields and thus direction $\mathbf{v}_0$ change.
$^1$H. Heintzmann, E. Schrüfer, Physics Letters A 43, 287––288 (1973).

General properties of asymptotic trajectories

If $$\mathbf{E} = \mathbf{f}(\mathbf{r}, t) - \mathbf{f}(\mathbf{r}, -t) $$ $$\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{g}(\mathbf{r}, t) + \mathbf{g}(\mathbf{r}, -t) $$ $$\mathbf{E}(t+T) = \mathbf{E}(t)$$ $$\mathbf{B}(t+T) = \mathbf{B}(t)$$ Then $$\langle\mathbf{v}_0\rangle_T = 0$$ $$\mathbf{r}(t+T) = \mathbf{r}(t)$$
Example: standing linearly-polarized monochromatic plane wave $$\mathbf{E}=\mathbf{y}_0a_0\cos(t)\cos(x)$$ $$\mathbf{B}=\mathbf{z}_0a_0\sin(t)\sin(x)$$
